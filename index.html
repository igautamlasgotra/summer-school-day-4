BMW M5 Evolution

The Legendary Evolution of BMW M5

Since its debut in 1984, the BMW M5 has evolved from a refined sports sedan into a high-performance monster that still retains daily drivability. Born from BMW's Motorsport division, the M5 set the benchmark for performance luxury sedans — a legacy that continues to roar on roads today

First Blood - E28 M5 (1984)

Based on the regular 5 Series, the E28 M5 hid a BMW M1 engine under its hood — a 3.5L inline-6 producing 286 hp. It looked humble but shocked anyone who challenged it. A true wolf in sheep's clothing.

The V8 Masterpiece - E39 M5 (1998)

The E39 M5 is considered one of the greatest sedans ever. It featured a 4.9L naturally aspirated V8 with 400 hp, mated to a 6-speed manual. Balanced, raw, and beautifully designed — it’s a collector’s dream today

The V10 Era - E60 M5 (2005)

This generation was insane. With a Formula 1-inspired 5.0L V10 engine churning out 507 hp and a screaming redline of 8,250 rpm, the E60 was raw, wild, and unpredictable. It brought the M5 into supercar territory

AWD Revolution - F90 M5 (2018)

The F90 M5 broke tradition by introducing all-wheel drive. But purists could rejoice — it had a switchable RWD mode! Powered by a 4.4L twin-turbo V8 producing 600+ hp, it was a performance and tech marvel

The Modern Marvel - G90 M5 (2024)

The latest M5, launching in 2024, embraces hybrid tech. Expected to produce over 700 hp, it combines eco-conscious engineering with mind-blowing performance. It’s a glimpse into the future of performance sedans
